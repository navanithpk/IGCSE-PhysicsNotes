1(a)(i): Calculate the Speed

Question: A car travels 4800 meters in 120 seconds. Calculate its speed.

We use the formula for speed:

\\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} \\]

Substituting the values:

\\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{4800}{120} = 40 \, \text{m/s} \\]

Final Answer: The speed of the car is \( 40 \, \text{m/s} \).

1(a)(ii): Gradient of the Velocity-Time Graph

Question: What does the gradient of a straight-line section on a velocity-time graph represent?

The gradient of a velocity-time graph gives the acceleration.

To calculate the gradient:

\\[ \text{Gradient} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} \\]

If the velocity increases from \( 50 \, \text{m/s} \) to \( 60 \, \text{m/s} \) over \( 10 \, \text{seconds} \):

\\[ \text{Acceleration} = \frac{60 - 50}{10} = 1 \, \text{m/s}^2 \\]

Final Answer: The gradient represents an acceleration of \( 1 \, \text{m/s}^2 \).

1(a)(iii): Acceleration at \( t = 20 \, \text{s} \) and \( t = 100 \, \text{s} \)

Question: Describe the acceleration at \( t = 20 \, \text{s} \) and \( t = 100 \, \text{s} \).

- At \( t = 20 \, \text{s} \): The graph shows that acceleration is greater than zero. This means the object is speeding up.

- At \( t = 100 \, \text{s} \): The graph shows a flat line (constant velocity). Hence, acceleration is zero.

Final Answer:
\\[ \text{At } t = 20 \, \text{s}: \text{Acceleration} > 0 \\] \\[ \text{At } t = 100 \, \text{s}: \text{Acceleration} = 0 \\]

1(b)(i): Calculate the Force

Question: A car with a mass of \( 5.6 \times 10^5 \, \text{kg} \) accelerates at \( 0.75 \, \text{m/s}^2 \). Find the force.

We use Newton's Second Law:

\\[ F = ma \\]

Substitute the values:

\\[ F = 5.6 \times 10^5 \times 0.75 = 4.2 \times 10^5 \, \text{N} \\]

Final Answer: The force acting on the car is \( 4.2 \times 10^5 \, \text{N} \).

1(b)(ii): Describe the Motion

Question: What happens to the speed/velocity of the car?

The car is slowing down, meaning its speed decreases over time. This is due to negative acceleration (deceleration).

Final Answer: The car's velocity decreases with time.
