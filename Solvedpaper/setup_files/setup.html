The gravitational potential energy \( E \) is given by:

\[ E = mgh \]

where \( m \) is mass, \( g \) is acceleration due to gravity, and \( h \) is height.

The work done \( W \) is calculated using:

\[ W = F \cdot d \cdot \cos \theta \]

where \( F \) is force, \( d \) is displacement, and \( \theta \) is the angle between them.

Mechanics} $$ $$ \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} $$ $$ \text{Acceleration} = \frac{\text{Change in velocity}}{\text{Time}} $$ $$ v = u + at $$ \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \) $$ v^2 = u^2 + 2as $$ $$ \textbf{2. Forces and Momentum} $$ $$ \text{Force} = \text{Mass} \times \text{Acceleration} \quad (F = ma) $$ $$ \text{Weight} = \text{Mass} \times \text{Gravitational field strength} \quad (W = mg) $$ $$ \text{Momentum} = \text{Mass} \times \text{Velocity} \quad (p = mv) $$ $$ \text{Impulse} = \text{Change in momentum} \quad (F\Delta t = \Delta p) $$ $$ \textbf{3. Work, Energy, and Power} $$ $$ \text{Work done} = \text{Force} \times \text{Distance} \quad (W = Fd) $$ $$ \text{Kinetic Energy} = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 $$ $$ \text{Gravitational Potential Energy} = mgh $$ $$ \text{Power} = \frac{\text{Work done}}{\text{Time}} \quad (P = \frac{W}{t}) $$ $$ \text{Efficiency} = \frac{\text{Useful energy output}}{\text{Total energy input}} \times 100 $$
