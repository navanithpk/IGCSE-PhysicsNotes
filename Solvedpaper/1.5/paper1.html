\(\textbf{Air Resistance}\) should be written on the dotted line above the upward force in the diagram.

(a) At \(time = 10 s\), the skydiver is accelerating, meaning the weight force is greater than the air resistance. A reasonable suggestion for the upward force on the skydiver would be any value less than \(750 N.\)
(b) Between \(time = 20 s\) and \(time = 40 s\), the skydiver is falling at a constant speed. This means the forces are balanced. For the forces to be balanced, the upward force must be equal to the downward force. Therefore, the value of the upward force on the skydiver at \(time = 30 s\) is \(\textbf{750 N}.\)
