1.1 Physical Quantities and Measurement Techniques

Paper 1

Measurement and Basic Physics Calculations

In this section, we will explore measurement techniques and calculations involving pressure, volume, area, and time. Questions will test the understanding of fundamental concepts with real-world scenarios.

Problems

  • Question 1

    A device for measuring gas pressure is connected to a gas supply as shown in the diagram.


    (a) Determine the difference \( h \) between the mercury levels shown in the diagram.





    \( h = \, \ldots \, \text{mm} \) [2]

    The difference \( h \) between the mercury levels is calculated as:
    \( h = 242 \, \text{mm} - 82 \, \text{mm} = 160 \, \text{mm} \)
    Answer: \( 160 \, \text{mm} \)

    (b) The atmospheric pressure is \( 760 \, \text{mm} \) of mercury. Determine the pressure of the gas supply.





    \(\text{Pressure of gas supply} = \, \ldots \, \text{mm of mercury} \) [1]

    The pressure of the gas supply is calculated using the atmospheric pressure of \( 760 \, \text{mm} \):
    \( \text{Pressure of gas supply} = 760 + 160 = 920 \, \text{mm of mercury} \)
    Answer: \( 920 \, \text{mm of mercury} \)

  • Question 3

    The diagram shows a measuring cylinder containing some water.


    (a) State the volume of the water in the measuring cylinder.





    \( \text{Volume} = \, \ldots \, \text{cm}^3 \) [1]

    Answer: \( 21 \, \text{cm}^3 \) [1]

    (b) A student adds \( 20 \) drops of water to the cylinder. The new volume is \( 25 \, \text{cm}^3 \). Calculate the average volume of one drop of water.





    \( \text{Average volume of one drop} = \, \ldots \, \text{cm}^3 \) [4]

    The new volume of the cylinder after adding 20 drops is \( 25 \, \text{cm}^3 \), so the increase in volume is:
    \( \Delta V = 25 - 21 = 4.0 \, \text{cm}^3 \)
    The average volume of one drop is:
    \( \frac{\Delta V}{\text{number of drops}} = \frac{4.0}{20} = 0.20 \, \text{cm}^3 \)
    Answer: \( 0.20 \, \text{cm}^3 \)

    [Total: 5]

  • Question 5

    The diagram shows a metal block and its dimensions. Calculate the area of the block in contact with the ground.





    \( \text{Area} = \, \ldots \, \text{cm}^2 \) [2]

    The area of the block in contact with the ground is:
    \( \text{Area} = \text{length} \times \text{width} = 12 \, \text{cm} \times 2.0 \, \text{cm} = 24 \, \text{cm}^2 \)
    Answer: \( 24 \, \text{cm}^2 \)

    [Total: 2]

  • Question 6

    The diagram shows a closed textbook with \( 270 \) sheets. The total thickness is \( 29 \, \text{mm} \). Calculate the average thickness of one sheet of paper.





    \( \text{Average thickness of one sheet} = \, \ldots \, \text{mm} \) [3]

    The total thickness of the book is \( 29 \, \text{mm} \), and the number of sheets is \( 270 \):
    \( \text{Average thickness} = \frac{\text{total thickness}}{\text{number of sheets}} = \frac{29}{270} = 0.11 \, \text{mm} \)
    Answer: \( 0.11 \, \text{mm} \)

  • Question 8

    Water is dripping from a tap into a measuring cylinder.
    The water drops fall from the tap at equal time intervals.
    A student uses a stop-watch to measure the time taken for the tap to produce \( 200 \) drops. The diagram shows the time reading on the stop-watch.

    (a) Determine the time for \( 200 \) drops.





    \( \text{Time} = \, \ldots \, \text{s} \) [2]

    The total time for \( 200 \) drops is calculated as:
    \( \text{Total time} = 180 + 46.5 = 226.5 \, \text{s} \)
    Answer: \( 226.5 \, \text{s} \)

    (b) Calculate the average time interval between drops.



    \( \text{Time interval} = \, \ldots \, \text{s} \) [2]

    The average time interval between drops is:
    \( \text{Time interval} = \frac{\text{total time}}{\text{number of drops}} = \frac{226.5}{200} = 1.1 \, \text{s} \)
    Answer: \( 1.1 \, \text{s} \)

    [Total: 4]

    The diagram shows a dripping tap and a measuring cylinder. The water drops all have the same volume.
    The drops fall from the tap at equal time intervals.

    The student collects \( 200 \) of the drops in a measuring cylinder. The total volume collected is \( 60 \, \text{cm}^3 .
    Calculate the average volume of one drop of water.



    Volume = \( \, \ldots \, \text{cm}^3 \)

    \( \text{Average volume} = \frac{\text{total volume}}{\text{number of drops}} = \frac{60}{200} = 0.30 \, \text{cm}^3 \)
    Answer: \( 0.30 \, \text{cm}^3 \)

  • Question 1

    A train of mass \( 5.6 \times 10^5 \, \text{kg} \) is at rest in a station...
    At time t = 0 s, a resultant force acts on the train and it starts to accelerate forwards.
    The graph is the distance-time graph for the train for the first 120 s.

    (a) (i) Use the distance-time graph to determine the average speed of the train during the 120s.

    Average speed = .............................................. [1]

    Average speed = 40 m/s

    (a) (ii) Use the distance-time graph to determine the speed of the train at time \( t = 100s \).

    Speed = .............................................. [2]

    Speed = 50 to 60 m/s (gradient of the straight line)

    (a) (iii) Describe how the acceleration of the train at time \( t = 100s \) differs from the acceleration at time \( t = 20s \).

    ........................................................................... [2]

    At \( t = 20s \), acceleration > zero. At \( t = 100s \), acceleration = 0.

    (b) (i) The initial acceleration of the train is \( 0.75 \, \text{m/s}^2 \). Calculate the resultant force that acts on the train at this time.

    Resultant force = .............................................. [2]

    Resultant force = \( 5.6 \times 10^5 \times 0.75 = 4.2 \times 10^5 \, \text{N} \)

    (b) (ii) At time \( t = 120s \), the train begins to decelerate. State what is meant by deceleration.

    ........................................................................... [1]

    Deceleration means a decrease in speed over time.

    The fastest car is car A, and the slowest car is car B.

    (a) (ii) Without calculation, identify the fastest car and the slowest car.
    Complete the table.

    Speed = ............................................................. m/s [3]

    Speed = \( 50 \, \text{m} \div 4 \, \text{s} = 12.5 \, \text{m/s} \)

    (b) (i) Estimate the time, in minutes, for car C to travel 5000m.

    Estimated time = ............................ minutes [2]

    Estimated time = \( \frac{5000 \, \text{m}}{12.5 \, \text{m/s}} = 400 \, \text{seconds} = 6.67 \, \text{minutes} \)

    (b) (ii) Explain why your answer in (b)(i) may not be the same as the actual time taken for the car to travel 5000m.

    ........................................................................... [2]

    The car may experience speed changes due to road conditions, traffic, or other factors.
